Your Structured Content
Machine-Readable Intelligence

HTML + Schema from Your Architecture:

<article class="component component--program-card" data-post-id="8" data-post-type="program"> <h2>Executive MBA</h2> <dl class="program-metadata"> <dt>Duration</dt><dd>18 months</dd> <dt>Format</dt><dd>Hybrid</dd> <dt>Level</dt><dd>Executive</dd> </dl> </article> <!-- Schema.org markup --> <script type="application/ld+json"> { "@type": "Course", "name": "Executive MBA", "timeRequired": "P18M", "courseMode": "Hybrid", "audience": "Executive" } </script> <article class="component component--testimonial" data-post-id="123" data-post-type="testimonial"> <blockquote>Great program</blockquote> <cite> <a href="/person/sarah-chen">Sarah Chen</a>, CEO, <a href="/organization/techcorp">TechCorp</a> </cite> </article> <script type="application/ld+json"> { "@type": "Review", "reviewBody": "Great program", "author": { "@type": "Person", "@id": "/person/sarah-chen", "name": "Sarah Chen", "jobTitle": "CEO", "worksFor": { "@type": "Organization", "@id": "/organization/techcorp", "name": "TechCorp" } }, "itemReviewed": { "@type": "Course", "@id": "/program/executive-mba" } } </script>
✅ What Qualified Understands
"This is a Course entity called 'Executive MBA' that takes 18 months in a hybrid format for executive-level participants. Sarah Chen is a Person entity who is CEO at TechCorp (an Organization entity). Sarah gave a Review of the Executive MBA program. I can link to Sarah's full profile and TechCorp's page. I know the relationships: Sarah works-for TechCorp, Sarah reviewed Executive MBA, Sarah's role is CEO."
✅ Semantic HTML structure ✅ Clear entity types (Course, Person, Organization) ✅ Explicit relationships ✅ Schema.org markup ✅ Qualified knows facts with certainty

How Qualified Uses Your Structured Content

When your architecture generates machine-readable content, Qualified can deliver intelligent, contextual conversations

1

Crawl & Parse

Qualified crawls your site and finds:
  • Custom Post Types with clean HTML structure
  • Schema.org markup declaring entity types (Course, Person, Organization, Event)
  • Semantic HTML with <article>, <dl>, proper headings
  • Relationship links connecting People → Organizations → Programs
Result: Qualified builds a knowledge graph of your content
2

Understand Context

From schema markup and metadata, Qualified knows:
  • "Executive MBA" is a Course (not just text)
  • "Sarah Chen" is a Person who is an alumni (role taxonomy)
  • "TechCorp" is an Organization in the Technology industry
  • Sarah works for TechCorp (relationship)
  • Sarah reviewed Executive MBA (relationship)
Result: Qualified understands relationships, not just keywords
3

Answer Intelligently

When a visitor asks a question, Qualified can:
  • Find relevant Programs by filtering metadata (level: executive, format: hybrid)
  • Surface testimonials from specific industries (technology sector CEOs)
  • Recommend faculty who teach in specific departments (strategy)
  • Suggest events featuring speakers from tech companies
Result: Precise, contextual responses instead of keyword matching
4

Surface Related Content

Because content is relational, Qualified can say:
  • "Sarah Chen also spoke at our AI Strategy Summit" (Person → Event relationship)
  • "Other TechCorp executives have attended our programs" (Organization → Person → Program)
  • "Prof. Wade who teaches this course authored 15 articles on digital transformation" (Person → Article relationship)
Result: Conversational depth that feels human

How Schema Markup Powers AI Understanding
